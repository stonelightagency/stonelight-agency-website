Unique Recessional Songs to End Your Ceremony on a High Note

Published on May 23, 2025 by Keys by Caleb

The ceremony is over, you've said "I do," and now it's time to celebrate! The recessional song is your victory lap, your first moments as a married couple, and the official kickoff to the party. While the processional is often about tender emotion, the recessional is all about joy, energy, and personality. Ditch the traditional and consider one of these unique recessional songs, all of which sound fantastic as instrumental piano pieces.

Upbeat & Fun

Get your guests smiling and tapping their feet as you walk back up the aisle.

  • "This Will Be (An Everlasting Love)" by Natalie Cole: Pure, infectious joy. It’s impossible not to feel happy listening to this classic.
  • "You Make My Dreams (Come True)" by Hall & Oates: An upbeat and instantly recognizable tune that screams celebration.
  • "Happy" by Pharrell Williams: The title says it all. A modern, fun choice to get the good vibes flowing.
  • "Lovely Day" by Bill Withers: A soulful, positive song that sets a wonderful tone for the rest of the celebration.

Classic & Cool

For a timeless feel that's still full of personality.

  • "Signed, Sealed, Delivered I'm Yours" by Stevie Wonder: A Motown classic with a message that's perfectly on point.
  • "Don't Stop Me Now" by Queen: For the couple ready to take on the world. It’s a bold, triumphant choice.
  • "Here Comes The Sun" by The Beatles: A beautiful and optimistic song, perfect for a bright beginning. An instrumental piano version is especially stunning.

Indie & Alternative

For the couple whose tastes lean a little more off the beaten path.

  • "Home" by Edward Sharpe & The Magnetic Zeros: A quirky, heartfelt anthem about finding your home in each other.
  • "Ho Hey" by The Lumineers: Simple, sweet, and wonderfully catchy. It encourages a sing-along spirit.
  • "First Day of My Life" by Bright Eyes: A more tender, yet incredibly optimistic and beautiful choice for a heartfelt exit.

Pro-Tip: The Power of the Piano

Any of these songs can be arranged for solo piano, giving them a unique blend of elegance and energy. A live pianist can adjust the tempo as you walk, building the energy and creating a truly custom moment. It’s a great way to use a popular song while still making it feel distinct and personal to your wedding.

Your recessional is the perfect place to let your personalities shine. Choose a song that makes you both want to dance your way into your new life together!
